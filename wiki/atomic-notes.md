**One idea per page is what makes a vault computable — precisely linkable,
deduplicatable, and contradiction-checkable.**

A page holding five ideas can't be linked precisely (which idea does the link
point at?), can't be deduped when a new source overlaps one of the five, and
hides contradictions inside its own prose. Splitting to one idea per page
makes every connection explicit and every claim individually checkable —
which is what [[connection-maintenance]] and [[wiki-linting]] operate on.

Two companion rules keep atoms useful:

- **Owner's voice, not the source's.** Rewriting forces understanding;
  a pasted quote is storage, not knowledge.
- **Never lose attribution.** Every atom records where it came from, so any
  claim can be audited back to its source when it's later disputed or
  superseded.

Rule of thumb in this vault: a source yielding five ideas becomes five pages,
cross-linked.
