**In May 2022 the algorithmic stablecoin UST lost its dollar peg and its
sister token LUNA hyperinflated from $80 to fractions of a cent in a week —
roughly $40-60B erased, and the detonator for that cycle's entire credit
system.**

The design was reflexive: UST held $1 because it could always be swapped
for $1 of newly minted LUNA. That works while LUNA has value and confidence
holds — it is a peg backed by belief in the peg. Anchor Protocol paying
~20% "risk-free" yield on UST pulled in ~$14B of deposits and made the
structure enormous before it was tested. When large UST withdrawals broke
confidence, redemption minted LUNA exponentially (death spiral), and both
legs went to zero together.

The contagion mattered as much as the collapse: funds and lenders holding
UST/LUNA — Three Arrows Capital, Celsius, Voyager, BlockFi — fell like
dominoes over the following months, and the chain ended at
[[ftx-collapse]] in November. Do Kwon was arrested (2023) and faced US
fraud charges. Regulatory consequence: "algorithmic" stablecoins became
politically radioactive — the US GENIUS Act and EU MiCA
([[crypto-regulation]]) effectively mandate the fully-reserved model of
[[stablecoins]]. Standing lesson: any yield without a visible source *is*
the risk, and pegs are only as strong as their worst day.
