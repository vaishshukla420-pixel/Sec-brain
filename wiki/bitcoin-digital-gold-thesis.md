**The dominant investment case for bitcoin: a provably scarce, seizure- and
debasement-resistant store of value — "digital gold" — absorbing a slice of
the world's ~$20T+ store-of-value demand. Partly validated, not proven.**

The argument: 21M fixed supply ([[bitcoin]]) vs endless fiat issuance;
portable and divisible where gold is not; custody-able by anyone
([[self-custody]]); and now institutionally wrapped ([[bitcoin-spot-etf]]).
Sovereign and corporate adoption ([[institutional-adoption]], the US
strategic reserve, treasury companies) strengthens the Lindy story each
cycle it survives.

Evidence for: surviving every drawdown and obituary since 2009; ETF-era
allocation by pensions and sovereigns; increasing correlation with gold
during debasement scares. Evidence against, kept honest: volatility still
far exceeds a store of value's job description; in acute crises it has
traded like a risk asset (March 2020, 2022), not a haven; its "digital
gold" behavior is regime-dependent and young; and the long-term security
budget ([[proof-of-work]] fee transition) is an unresolved structural
question. The thesis is *reflexive* — it becomes truer as more allocators
believe it, which is also how it could fail.

**Status of the claim:** strengthening through the 2024-26 institutional
era but unfalsified in both directions — revisit against
[[crypto-2026-state]] data each cycle.
