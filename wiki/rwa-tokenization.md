**Real-world-asset tokenization puts traditional financial assets — first
and foremost US Treasury funds — onto blockchains as transferable tokens,
and it is the sector where Wall Street stopped mocking crypto and started
shipping on it.**

The beachhead was cash equivalents: BlackRock's BUIDL tokenized money-market
fund (March 2024) and Franklin Templeton's on-chain fund turned "T-bill
yield, on-chain, settling 24/7" into a product, growing tokenized
Treasuries from ~$100M (early 2023) to multi-billions within two years.
Behind them queue private credit, commodities (tokenized gold), equities,
and funds — the thesis being that a token settling in seconds against
[[stablecoins]] beats T+2 settlement, reconciliation departments, and
market hours.

Professional skepticism to retain: the token is only as good as the *legal
wrapper* — you own a claim enforced by courts and custodians, not the
asset by cryptography, so RWA reintroduces exactly the trusted
intermediaries [[bitcoin]] was built to remove (the design trade is
deliberate: efficiency, not trustlessness). Liquidity is also nascent —
tokenizing an illiquid asset does not make it liquid, it makes it a
tokenized illiquid asset. Still, this plus stablecoins is the credible
"tokenization of everything" path institutions are actually walking
([[institutional-adoption]]), mostly on [[ethereum]] and its L2s, with the
ownership records themselves being [[nfts]] in mechanism.
