**Irreversible payments plus pseudonymity plus unsophisticated buyers make
crypto the most efficient scam substrate ever built — and scam revenue
(tens of billions yearly) rivals legitimate sector revenues.**

The taxonomy worth knowing:

- **Rug pulls:** insiders launch a token, pump it, dump on buyers or yank
  the liquidity. Endemic in [[memecoins]]; enabled by frictionless issuance.
- **Pig butchering:** long-con romance/investment grooming into fake
  trading platforms; industrial-scale operations run from compounds in
  Southeast Asia with trafficked labor — a genuine humanitarian crime, and
  by dollar volume among the largest fraud categories on earth.
- **Wallet drainers:** phishing kits that get victims to sign a malicious
  approval; one signature empties the wallet ([[self-custody]]'s sharp edge).
- **Ponzi yield:** "guaranteed" returns paid from new deposits — from
  BitConnect (2017) to Celsius (2022); [[terra-luna-collapse]]'s 20% Anchor
  yield was the systemic version.
- **Fake exchanges/airdrops, address poisoning, SIM swaps** — the retail
  long tail.

Defense heuristics that generalize: unexplained yield is the product;
urgency is the tell; verify contracts and domains character-by-character;
and approvals are standing permissions — revoke them. Scams differ from
[[crypto-hacks]] in that the victim signs willingly; the countermeasure is
skepticism, not cryptography.
