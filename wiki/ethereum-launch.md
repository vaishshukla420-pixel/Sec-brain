**In 2013 a 19-year-old Bitcoin Magazine writer proposed making the
blockchain programmable; by 30 July 2015 Ethereum was live — and crypto
stopped being only about money.**

Vitalik Buterin's pitch: Bitcoin's scripting is deliberately crippled;
build a chain with a general-purpose virtual machine and let people deploy
arbitrary [[smart-contracts]]. The project crowdfunded ~$18M in mid-2014 by
selling ETH for BTC — itself a preview of the [[ico-boom]] funding model it
would later host — and launched with a genesis allocation to ~9,000
crowdsale buyers plus the foundation, a *premine* that Bitcoin purists
still cite as a cultural dividing line between the two chains
([[bitcoin-genesis]] had no such allocation).

Nearly everything the market later called a sector is downstream of this
launch: token standards (ERC-20) enabled the ICO era, composable contracts
enabled [[defi]], ERC-721 enabled [[nfts]], and the competitor chains of
[[layer-1-platforms]] are all answers to "Ethereum, but faster." Its first
existential test came within a year — [[the-dao-hack]].
