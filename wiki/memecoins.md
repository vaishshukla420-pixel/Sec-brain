**Memecoins are tokens whose only product is attention — speculation
stripped of any utility pretense — and in the 2024-25 cycle they became,
by trading volume, one of crypto's largest sectors and its bluntest
mirror.**

Dogecoin (2013) was the joke that never died; the modern era
industrialized it: Solana's pump.fun (2024) reduced token launch to a
meme and a click, spawning millions of tokens whose lifecycle — launch,
influencer pump, insider dump — compresses the [[ico-boom]] into hours.
The apex was political: the TRUMP token (January 2025) minted tens of
billions in paper value days before a US presidential inauguration,
erasing any remaining line between memecoins, politics, and
[[crypto-regulation]] conflict-of-interest questions.

The honest analysis: memecoins are lottery-ticket markets with power-law
outcomes, insider advantages ([[tokenomics]] doesn't apply — there is no
model, only distribution and timing), and rug-pull rates that make them
[[crypto-scams]]' native habitat. But they are also *revealed preference*:
the market repeatedly chooses pure gambling over utility narratives, which
tells you something true about crypto's current retail demand — and their
volume bootstrapped real infrastructure (Solana's fee economy,
[[layer-1-platforms]]). Professionals track them as a sentiment gauge:
memecoin froth marks late-cycle [[crypto-market-cycles]] territory.
