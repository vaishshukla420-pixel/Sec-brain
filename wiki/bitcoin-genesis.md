**On 31 October 2008 — six weeks after Lehman fell — the pseudonymous
Satoshi Nakamoto published a nine-page whitepaper; on 3 January 2009 the
first block went live carrying a newspaper headline about bank bailouts.**

The genesis block's embedded message — *"The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor
on brink of second bailout for banks"* — is both a timestamp and a mission
statement: money that doesn't depend on the institutions then failing. The
whitepaper's contribution was solving double-spending without a central
party, by combining the pieces of [[pre-bitcoin-digital-cash]] into a
working system ([[proof-of-work]] + peer-to-peer [[blockchain]]).

Milestones of the bootstrap era: first transaction to Hal Finney (January
2009); the famous 10,000-BTC pizza (22 May 2010) — the first real-world
price discovery; Satoshi's disappearance (2011), leaving ~1M mined coins
untouched — an absence that became a feature, since Bitcoin has no founder
to subpoena, lobby, or dox. Anonymity of the founder plus the bailout
headline give [[bitcoin]] a founding mythology no later chain has, and the
untouched Satoshi coins remain one of crypto's standing what-ifs.
