**Crypto has moved in roughly four-year boom-bust cycles — historically
anchored to Bitcoin's halvings — where each mania is driven by a new
narrative, ends in a spectacular blowup, and bottoms ~75-85% down before
the next build-out.**

The observed pattern: halving tightens new [[bitcoin]] supply → bitcoin
rallies → capital rotates outward into higher-risk assets (the "risk
curve": ETH → large caps → [[memecoins]]) → leverage and euphoria peak
alongside a signature narrative → a credit event pops it → long "crypto
winter." Cycle One peaked 2013 (~$1.1k BTC); Cycle Two peaked December 2017
(~$19.7k, the [[ico-boom]]) and bottomed ~84% down; Cycle Three peaked
November 2021 (~$69k, [[defi]]/[[nft-mania]]) and unwound through the
cascading frauds of 2022 ([[terra-luna-collapse]], [[ftx-collapse]], ~77%
drawdown); Cycle Four followed the April 2024 halving with the
[[bitcoin-spot-etf]] institutional bid — see [[crypto-2026-state]] for
where that cycle stands now.

Treat the four-year clock as a *claim, not a law*: the sample size is tiny,
each cycle's driver was different, and the ETF era introduced a structurally
new buyer whose flows don't care about halvings. What repeats reliably is
the mechanism — leverage amplifying a narrative until a credit event —
not the calendar.
