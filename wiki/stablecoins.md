**Stablecoins are blockchain tokens pegged to a fiat currency (almost always
the US dollar) — crypto's killer app for payments, and the industry's main
bridge into the traditional financial system.**

Three designs, with very different risk:

- **Fiat-backed** (Tether USDT, Circle USDC): issuer holds dollars and
  T-bills, token is a redeemable IOU. Simple, dominant — and a custodial,
  centralized trust point that can freeze addresses.
- **Crypto-collateralized** (MakerDAO/Sky DAI): overcollateralized by
  on-chain assets, managed by [[smart-contracts]]. Decentralized but
  capital-inefficient.
- **Algorithmic** (uncollateralized, peg held by incentive games): the
  design that failed catastrophically — see [[terra-luna-collapse]].

Stablecoins settle trillions of dollars a year, dwarf every other crypto
use case in real-world adoption (remittances, dollar access in weak-currency
countries, exchange settlement), and earn issuers billions in T-bill yield —
which is why banks and fintechs piled in once the US GENIUS Act (2025)
created a federal licensing regime ([[crypto-regulation]]). They are also
where crypto most directly touches systemic finance: a large depeg is a
money-market run on-chain.
