**Ethereum generalized the blockchain from "shared ledger of money" to
"shared world computer": anyone can deploy programs (smart contracts) that
hold and move value by themselves — which is what made DeFi, NFTs, and
stablecoins possible.**

Proposed by Vitalik Buterin (2013), launched July 2015
([[ethereum-launch]]). Its Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) executes
[[smart-contracts]]; users pay "gas" in ETH for computation, and since
EIP-1559 (2021) most of that fee is burned, tying ETH's supply to usage.
The Merge (September 2022) moved consensus to [[proof-of-stake]], making
ETH a staked, yield-bearing asset.

Ethereum's scaling strategy is deliberate: keep the base layer maximally
secure and decentralized, push volume to [[layer-2-scaling]] rollups, and
serve those rollups cheap data (blob transactions since the Dencun upgrade,
March 2024). It hosts the deepest [[defi]] ecosystem and most
[[rwa-tokenization]], while faster monolithic rivals ([[layer-1-platforms]],
chiefly Solana) compete on raw throughput and cost. The recurring bear case:
L2s capture the users and fees while the base layer earns less — the
"value accrual" debate.
