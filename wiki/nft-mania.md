**In 2021-22 NFTs went from curiosity to a ~$25B/year market — Beeple's
$69M Christie's sale, Bored Apes as celebrity status symbols — then lost
over 95% of volume in the bust: crypto's purest pure-attention bubble.**

Non-fungible tokens (ERC-721, 2018) made blockchain entries *unique*, so a
token could represent one specific thing — an image, a ticket, a domain.
The mania phase priced profile pictures as Veblen goods: CryptoPunks and
Bored Ape Yacht Club floor prices hit hundreds of thousands of dollars,
OpenSea briefly did >$5B/month (January 2022), brands and A-listers piled
in. The bust was proportionate — by 2023 most collections traded ~90-99%
down, and studies found the majority effectively worthless.

The knowledge worth keeping is the separation the bust performed: *NFT as
speculative art asset* mostly died; *NFT as technical primitive* — a
transferable, on-chain record of unique ownership — quietly persisted in
tickets, gaming assets, domains, and the ownership rails of
[[rwa-tokenization]]. Mania-phase artifacts also fed the fraud economy
(wash trading, rug-pull mints — [[crypto-scams]]). Cycle three's speculative
energy later reincarnated with even less pretense as [[memecoins]].
