**Layer-1s are the base blockchains competing to host the on-chain economy
— a design-space fight between Ethereum's security-first modularity and
Solana's speed-first integration, with a graveyard of "Ethereum killers"
behind them.**

The trilemma frames the trade: decentralization, security, scalability —
optimize two, sacrifice one. [[ethereum]] keeps its base layer conservative
and scales via [[layer-2-scaling]]. Solana integrates everything into one
fast chain (400ms slots, parallel execution, sub-cent fees), accepting
heavier hardware requirements and — in its early years — repeated outages
it has since largely engineered away; its low-cost UX made it the home of
[[memecoins]], consumer apps, and much of [[crypto-ai-intersection]]
activity, and this cycle it became Ethereum's first credible rival on real
usage. The 2021-22 cohort of alternatives (Avalanche, Cardano, the failed
VC-chain wave; Terra died outright — [[terra-luna-collapse]]) mostly
persists at reduced relevance.

How to evaluate any L1 like a professional: real fee revenue (not
subsidized TPS), validator count and hardware floor (decentralization),
developer retention, and whether its [[tokenomics]] emissions are buying
activity that would leave when incentives stop. History says L1 value
follows applications, not benchmarks — the chain that hosts the era's
killer app wins the cycle.
