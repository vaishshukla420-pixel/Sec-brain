**In mid-2020, Compound's COMP token launch ignited "yield farming" —
protocols paying users in their own tokens for deposits — and DeFi's locked
value went from under $1B to ~$180B in eighteen months.**

Two innovations converged. Uniswap's automated market maker (the x·y=k
constant-product formula) replaced order books with passive liquidity
pools, letting anyone market-make or list a token. Compound's liquidity
mining (June 2020) then showed that emitting governance tokens to users
could bootstrap billions in deposits overnight. The composability of
[[smart-contracts]] did the rest: yield strategies stacked protocol on
protocol ("money legos"), and food-named Uniswap forks (SushiSwap et al.)
cloned and vampire-attacked each other weekly.

What survived the inevitable unwind matters more than the mania: the AMM
DEX, permissionless lending pools, flash loans, and on-chain
[[stablecoins]] became durable financial infrastructure — the core of the
[[defi]] sector — while most farm tokens went to ~zero, a masterclass in
emissions-driven [[tokenomics]]. DeFi Summer set up cycle three's peak and
also its downfall: the leverage and yield promises it normalized were what
[[terra-luna-collapse]] and Celsius later industrialized.
