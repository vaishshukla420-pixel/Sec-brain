**Public blockchains are surveillance-perfect ledgers — every payment
visible forever — so a permanent sub-industry builds financial privacy on
top, and a permanent regulatory war tries to constrain it.**

The tools: **privacy coins** — Monero (ring signatures, default-private,
delisted from most regulated exchanges) and Zcash (zk-SNARKs, optional
shielding; notable market resurgence in late 2025 as privacy narratives
returned); **mixers** — Tornado Cash, a [[smart-contracts]] pool breaking
the link between deposit and withdrawal; and the maturing **zero-knowledge
proof** stack, which increasingly lets protocols prove compliance-relevant
facts (age, sanctions status, solvency) without revealing everything —
the likely long-term truce line, since it's the same math scaling
[[layer-2-scaling]] ZK-rollups.

The legal battlefront is the precedent-setter: OFAC sanctioned Tornado
Cash's *code itself* (August 2022) — the first sanctioning of software
rather than persons — then delisted it (March 2025) after courts ruled
immutable contracts aren't sanctionable "property"; developer prosecutions
(Storm, Pertsev) continued regardless. The tension is structural, not
resolvable: the same pool anonymizes a dissident's donations and a
[[crypto-hacks]] launderer's loot ([[crypto-regulation]] keeps relitigating
where to cut). Cypherpunk lineage note: privacy wasn't bolted onto crypto —
crypto came *from* the privacy movement ([[pre-bitcoin-digital-cash]]).
