**Rollups execute transactions cheaply off-chain and post compressed proofs
back to Ethereum, inheriting its security while multiplying its capacity —
the bet that a blockchain should scale like a tree, not a monolith.**

Two proof families: **optimistic rollups** (Arbitrum, Optimism, Coinbase's
Base) assume validity and allow fraud challenges during a ~7-day window;
**ZK rollups** (zkSync, Starknet, Scroll) post cryptographic validity
proofs — instant finality, harder engineering, and the presumed end-state.
Ethereum's Dencun upgrade (March 2024) gave rollups dedicated cheap "blob"
data, cutting L2 fees to cents ([[ethereum]]'s roadmap continues to add
blob capacity).

What a professional watches: **L2Beat's risk stages** — most L2s still run
training wheels (centralized sequencers, upgradable contracts, security
councils), so "inherits Ethereum security" is aspiration until Stage 2;
**value fragmentation** — liquidity split across dozens of L2s with bridges
between them ([[crypto-hacks]] territory); and the **fee-accrual debate** —
Base's success proves the model can print money for its operator while
paying Ethereum pennies, feeding the bear case on L1 value capture. Base
(launched 2023) becoming the dominant L2 by activity also prefigures the
next structural fight: corporate chains on public rails
([[institutional-adoption]]).
