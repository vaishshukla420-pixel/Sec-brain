**As of 11 July 2026 crypto is in a confirmed late-stage bear market —
total market cap $2.29T, down ~47% from the October 2025 peak of $4.27T —
while the regulatory and institutional plumbing keeps advancing straight
through the drawdown.** *(Deliberately dated snapshot: supersede with
`**Update:**` lines or a successor page at the next regime change.)*

**Prices and cycle.** BTC ~$64.4k, -49% from its $126,080 all-time high
(6 October 2025); dominance 56%. ETH ~$1.8k, -64% from its new record
($4,954, August 2025). H1 2026 was bitcoin's second-worst first half ever
(-27.7%), bottoming (so far) ~$59k in early June. The cycle mapped
eerily onto [[crypto-market-cycles]]: peak arrived 17.6 months
post-halving (prior cycle: 18.0). Analyst consensus — Cantor Fitzgerald
(bottoms average 384 days after peak → ~October 2026), Glassnode
(long-term-holder capitulation at levels last seen December 2022, "bottom
building") — puts the low in Q4 2026, with model targets in the $47-55k
zone. No credible voice is calling a new bull leg yet; the debate is
"bottoming now" versus "one more leg down."

**What broke.** The 10 October 2025 tariff shock triggered the largest
liquidation in crypto history ($19.4B across 1.6M traders in 24h). A
hawkish Fed under new chair Warsh (rates 3.50-3.75%, hike risk priced,
DXY back above 100) removed the macro bid. [[bitcoin-spot-etf]] flows
reversed — June 2026 was the worst outflow month on record (~$4B; YTD
-$5.5B, though cumulative flows remain +$51B) — and the leveraged
treasury-company complex went into forced-selling reverse, Strategy
included ([[institutional-adoption]]). A Mt. Gox distribution deadline
(October 2026) still overhangs supply.

**What held or grew.** [[stablecoins]] supply hit ~$311B (up from ~$200B
in January 2025) with Circle federally chartered. Tokenized Treasuries
reached $15.6B — up ~3-4x in eighteen months ([[rwa-tokenization]]).
Solana ETFs (October 2025) and XRP ETFs (November 2025) launched under
generic listing standards ([[crypto-regulation]]). [[defi]] TVL sits at
$74.5B (-58% from ATH) but the machinery works: Hyperliquid alone runs
~32% of perp-DEX volume, and lending overtook liquid staking as the top
category. Solana out-trades Ethereum L1 on DEX volume ($63.6B vs $28.7B
per 30d) and out-earns it in native fees, while Ethereum keeps the
capital ($40.6B TVL vs $4.9B) and, post-Fusaka blob scaling (December
2025), its [[layer-2-scaling]] fees run sub-cent. Miners are becoming AI
companies: >$70B in HPC contracts signed; up to 70% of public-miner
revenue projected from AI by December ([[crypto-ai-intersection]] adjacent).
[[privacy-tech]] had its rotation (Zcash 10x, flipping Monero).
[[memecoins]] deflated -82% from the late-2024 peak; NFTs run ~5% of
their peak volume. Crime stayed industrial: $3.4B stolen in 2025 (Bybit
$1.5B; North Korea $2.0B), ~$1-1.3B in H1 2026 with Lazarus behind both
top incidents ([[crypto-hacks]]).

**The read.** Price and adoption have decoupled: the speculative layer is
deleveraging on schedule while the regulated-infrastructure layer
compounds. That divergence — not the drawdown itself — is the defining
fact of 2026, and the strongest data point yet for scoring the
[[bitcoin-digital-gold-thesis]] next cycle.
