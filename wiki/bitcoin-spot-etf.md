**On 10 January 2024, after a decade of rejections and a lost court case,
the SEC approved spot Bitcoin ETFs — and BlackRock's IBIT became the
fastest-growing ETF in history, rewiring who owns bitcoin and how the
market trades.**

The turn wasn't voluntary: Grayscale beat the SEC in court (2023), leaving
approval as the only consistent option. Eleven funds launched at once;
IBIT crossed $50B AUM within its first year — a record — and spot Ethereum
ETFs followed in July 2024. For the first time, retirement accounts,
RIAs, and institutions could hold BTC exposure through ordinary brokerage
rails, with custody handled by regulated giants ([[institutional-adoption]]).

Structural effects on the market: a persistent, price-insensitive
flow-driven buyer entered [[crypto-market-cycles]] (daily ETF net flows
became *the* watched statistic); volatility profiles began to resemble
other institutional assets; and bitcoin's identity shifted decisively
toward "macro asset" — a development the [[bitcoin-digital-gold-thesis]]
predicted and the [[self-custody]] purists mourn, since coins-in-an-ETF
are precisely *not* your keys. The ETF wrapper also became the template
regulators reused for the next wave of crypto ETFs
([[crypto-2026-state]]).
