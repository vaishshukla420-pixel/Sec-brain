**In November 2022 FTX — the second-largest exchange, fronted by the
industry's most trusted founder — imploded in nine days, revealing ~$8B of
customer funds secretly lent to its trading arm: not a crypto failure but
plain custodial fraud at crypto speed.**

The sequence: a CoinDesk leak showed Alameda Research's balance sheet was
mostly FTX's own FTT token (2 Nov); Binance dumped its FTT and announced,
then abandoned, a rescue (8-9 Nov); a withdrawal run found the vault empty;
Chapter 11 followed (11 Nov). Sam Bankman-Fried was convicted on seven
fraud counts (November 2023) and sentenced to 25 years (March 2024).
Politically, the man who had been crypto's face in Washington became its
biggest liability, freezing US regulatory progress for two years
([[crypto-regulation]]).

What it rhymes with: [[mt-gox-collapse]], mechanically — commingled
customer funds, insolvency masked until a run — which is why the industry
response targeted custody: proof-of-reserves attestations, segregated
custody mandates in MiCA and later US law, and a durable shift to
[[self-custody]]. Note the irony that pays forward: [[defi]] protocols,
whose reserves are on-chain by construction, processed the same November
2022 chaos without failing — transparent leverage broke gracefully;
hidden leverage broke catastrophically.
