**By mid-2026 institutional crypto has split in two: the *speculative*
institutional trade (leveraged bitcoin-treasury companies) is unwinding
painfully in the drawdown, while the *infrastructure* build-out (charters,
custody, tokenized deposits and funds) accelerated straight through it.**

The casualty side, July 2026: Strategy (ex-MicroStrategy) holds 843,775
BTC at an average cost of ~$75.5k — underwater at ~$60-64k spot — and has
become a *net seller* (its largest-ever sale, ~$216M, funded preferred
dividends in early July). The copycat treasury-company sector it spawned
(~130-200 public companies, ~1.2M BTC) lost ~$62B in market value; ~40% of
the top hundred trade below the value of their coins, and the weakest
(Nakamoto Holdings -99%) are forced sellers — a leverage machine running
in reverse, exactly the mechanism [[crypto-market-cycles]] predicts. Even
early-adopter allocators diverged: Abu Dhabi's Mubadala kept adding to its
[[bitcoin-spot-etf]] position (~$0.6-1B) while Wisconsin's pension board,
the celebrated first mover of 2024, exited entirely. The US Strategic
Bitcoin Reserve (~300k+ seized BTC) has bought nothing since its 2025
executive order and remains an inter-agency turf fight.

The build-out side, same months: Circle became the first federally
chartered stablecoin issuer (OCC, 10 July 2026), with Ripple, Fidelity,
Paxos and BitGo holding conditional charters; JPMorgan's JPMD deposit
token runs on a public L2 ([[layer-2-scaling]]'s Base) and the major US
banks are building a shared tokenized-deposit network; BNY launched
tokenized deposits; BlackRock's BUIDL fund (~$2.4B) anchors a ~$15B
tokenized-Treasury market ([[rwa-tokenization]]); total [[stablecoins]]
supply grew to ~$310-320B with $420B projected. The professional
takeaway: price adoption and rails adoption have *decoupled* — balance
sheets retreat, plumbing advances — and the plumbing is the durable
signal.
