**Bitcoin is the first cryptocurrency and still the anchor of the entire
market: a fixed-supply digital money (21 million coins, ever) secured by
proof-of-work, whose four-yearly issuance halvings pace the industry's
boom-bust rhythm.**

The protocol issues new BTC to miners on a hard-coded schedule that halves
every 210,000 blocks (~4 years): 50 → 25 (2012) → 12.5 (2016) → 6.25 (2020)
→ 3.125 (April 2024), asymptotically approaching 21 million total around
2140. That programmatic scarcity — no committee can print more — is the
core of its [[bitcoin-digital-gold-thesis]] and the reason halvings anchor
[[crypto-market-cycles]].

Bitcoin deliberately does *less* than later chains: no rich
[[smart-contracts]], ~10-minute blocks, small throughput. In exchange it has
the longest unbroken uptime (since January 2009 — [[bitcoin-genesis]]), the
deepest liquidity, the most conservative culture, and roughly half of the
entire crypto market's value ("dominance") in most years. Institutional
access arrived via the [[bitcoin-spot-etf]] in 2024, transforming who owns
it. Its open question is long-term security funding as the block subsidy
shrinks ([[proof-of-work]]).
