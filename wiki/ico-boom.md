**In 2017-18, projects raised over $20B by selling newly minted tokens
directly to the public — a Cambrian explosion of permissionless
fundraising in which the large majority of projects failed or were outright
scams.**

The mechanics: write a whitepaper, deploy an ERC-20 token on [[ethereum]],
sell it for ETH/BTC. No prospectus, no vesting, no accountability. It
funded a few real things (Ethereum itself was the prototype ICO;
Chainlink, Filecoin and EOS's $4B raise came from this era) and an ocean
of vapor — studies at the time estimated ~80% of 2017 ICOs were scams by
volume of projects. The mania drove [[crypto-market-cycles]] cycle two to
its December 2017 peak (~$19.7k BTC) and its ~84% collapse through 2018.

The regulatory aftermath shaped everything since: the SEC applied the
Howey test to token sales ([[crypto-regulation]]), sued dozens of issuers,
and pushed fundraising into private SAFTs and offshore structures — the
"low float, high FDV" VC-unlock model whose [[tokenomics]] pathologies
retail traders paid for in the next two cycles. The pattern — new issuance
primitive → mania → fraud → rules — is crypto's most reliable rhyme; it
repeated with [[nft-mania]] and again with [[memecoins]].
