**Tokenomics is a token's monetary policy and incentive design — supply,
issuance, unlocks, and value capture — and it usually explains a token's
price behavior better than the project's technology does.**

The questions that matter when reading any token:

- **Supply:** fixed ([[bitcoin]]'s 21M) or inflationary? What is circulating
  vs total — and who holds the difference?
- **Unlocks:** insider/VC allocations vest on schedules; large unlock dates
  are recurring, predictable sell pressure. "Low float, high FDV" launches
  (tiny circulating supply, huge fully-diluted value) structurally transfer
  value from retail buyers to insiders.
- **Value capture:** does usage actually accrue to the token (fee burn like
  [[ethereum]]'s EIP-1559, revenue sharing, collateral demand) or is the
  token economically decorative?
- **Incentives:** emissions that pay users to show up (yield farming —
  [[defi-summer]]) buy growth that evaporates when emissions end.

Reading a token's distribution and emission schedule is the crypto
equivalent of reading a company's cap table and dilution — skipping it is
how buyers of 2021-25 vintage tokens ended up funding insider exits
([[crypto-scams]] covers the outright malicious versions).
