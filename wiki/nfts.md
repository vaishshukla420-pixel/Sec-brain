**Post-mania, the NFT is best understood as a primitive, not a market: a
blockchain-native record of unique ownership that outlived the speculative
sector built on top of it.**

After the 2021-22 bubble ([[nft-mania]]) deflated 90%+, what remained
splits into distinct businesses: **digital collectibles** (a shrunken but
real art/PFP market centered on a few blue chips); **utility records** —
tickets, memberships, in-game assets, domain names (ENS), where the NFT is
plumbing rather than product; and **ownership rails for
[[rwa-tokenization]]**, where a unique token maps to a unique off-chain
asset (a property deed, a fund share class, an invoice). The primitive also
quietly powers identity and attestation experiments (soulbound tokens).

The professional read: valuing an NFT *project* is venture analysis
(team, IP, distribution — mostly they fail); valuing the *primitive* is
infrastructure analysis, and there it clearly won — every major chain,
wallet, and marketplace supports unique tokens as a first-class type, and
royalties/marketplace dynamics remain a live design fight. The mania's
lasting contribution was onboarding: NFTs taught a mainstream cohort what
[[self-custody]] and gas fees are, one JPEG at a time.
