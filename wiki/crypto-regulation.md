**As of mid-2026 crypto regulation has flipped from enforcement war to
rule-writing race: stablecoins have a US federal law (GENIUS) being
implemented, market-structure law (CLARITY) is stuck in the Senate, MiCA
just culled ~80% of Europe's crypto firms, and agencies are regulating
ahead of legislators everywhere.**

Where each front stands (July 2026):

- **US stablecoins — GENIUS Act** (signed 18 July 2025): six agencies (OCC,
  FDIC, NCUA, FinCEN, Treasury, OFAC) issued *proposed* rules Dec 2025-May
  2026; no licenses granted under it yet — the regime effectively lands
  2027. Meanwhile the OCC moved faster than the law: national trust
  charters for Circle (final approval 10 July 2026 — first federally
  chartered stablecoin issuer), Ripple, BitGo, Fidelity, Paxos; Tether
  entered the US via a compliant coin (USAT, Jan 2026, issued through
  Anchorage). See [[stablecoins]].
- **US market structure — CLARITY Act:** passed the House (2025), *not*
  the Senate; committee versions advanced May 2026 but 60 votes are
  missing, with the central Democratic demand being ethics limits on
  senior officials' — including the president's — crypto businesses.
  Agencies filled the gap: a joint SEC-CFTC token taxonomy (early 2026)
  created a "digital commodities" lane without Congress.
- **SEC under Atkins:** enforcement retreat (Coinbase, Binance, Kraken,
  Consensys cases dropped/dismissed 2025; Ripple ended), "Project Crypto"
  rulemaking, and generic ETF listing standards (Sept 2025) that
  mass-produced spot ETFs — Solana (Oct 2025), XRP (Nov 2025), more queued
  ([[institutional-adoption]]).
- **EU — MiCA:** the transition ended 1 July 2026 with only ~210-280 of
  1,200+ firms fully authorized — a compliance mass-extinction; USDT is
  effectively delisted for EEA retail (Tether never sought authorization),
  handing the EU market to USDC.
- **Elsewhere:** UK final rulebook June 2026 (regime fully live late 2027);
  Hong Kong issued its first two stablecoin licenses (April 2026 — HSBC and
  a Standard Chartered JV); UAE/Singapore consolidating as licensed hubs.
- **CBDC:** the US statutorily banned a Fed CBDC through 2030 (July 2026),
  doubling down on private stablecoins as the digital dollar ([[cbdc]]).

The through-line for analysis: *regulation decoupled from price* — the
2026 drawdown ([[crypto-2026-state]]) did not slow the rule-writing, which
is the strongest evidence yet that the institutional layer treats crypto
as permanent plumbing rather than a speculative episode
([[crypto-regulation]] history began with the DAO Report —
[[the-dao-hack]] — and each crisis since wrote another chapter:
[[ico-boom]] → Howey enforcement; [[terra-luna-collapse]] +
[[ftx-collapse]] → custody and stablecoin reserve law).
