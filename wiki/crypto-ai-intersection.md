**The crypto-AI sector bets that machine agents need machine money — that
AI agents transacting autonomously will prefer permissionless payment rails
and provable digital scarcity to card networks built for humans.**

The serious threads, separated from the froth:

- **Agent payments:** an AI agent can hold keys and pay per-request without
  a bank account; standards like x402 (HTTP-native [[stablecoins]] payments)
  and agent-wallet frameworks target machine-to-machine commerce — the
  most concrete thesis in the sector.
- **Decentralized compute/inference markets** (Render, Akash, Bittensor's
  incentivized model networks): crypto incentives aggregating GPU supply
  outside the hyperscalers — real usage, though small next to centralized
  cloud.
- **Provenance in a deepfake world:** blockchains as timestamped
  attestation for what's authentic ([[nfts]] mechanism, serious use).
- **DePIN** (decentralized physical infrastructure — Helium's wireless
  networks): token emissions bootstrapping hardware deployment, the
  physical-world cousin of the same incentive trick.

The froth: AI-agent [[memecoins]] (the GOAT/ai16z wave, late 2024) proved
demand for the *story* long before the infrastructure matured — standard
[[crypto-market-cycles]] behavior where narrative front-runs utility by
years. The evaluation question for any crypto-AI token: would the system
still work with a database and Stripe? If yes, the token is decoration
([[tokenomics]]).
